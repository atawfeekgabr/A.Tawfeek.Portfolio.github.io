Churn Project

Data cleaning, statistical summary and dashboards via Excel included during Intense Data Analytics Course provided by IntuCraft.

Goal

The purpose of this dashboard is to understand, predict, and ultimately reduce customer churn within a business or organization. Churn analysis involves examining historical data related to customer behavior, interactions, and characteristics to identify patterns and factors that contribute to churn. Overall, the goal of churn data analysis is to empower businesses with actionable insights and strategies to reduce customer churn, increase customer retention, and ultimately drive sustainable growth and profitability.

Objectives

The primary objectives of churn data analysis include: 1- Understanding Churn Patterns: The first step in churn analysis is to gain insights into why customers are leaving. By analyzing historical churn data, businesses can identify common patterns, trends, and triggers associated with customer churn. This understanding helps in pinpointing areas of improvement and developing targeted retention strategies.
2- Predicting Future Churn: One of the key goals of churn data analysis is to develop predictive models that forecast which customers are at risk of churning in the future. Predictive analytics techniques, such as machine learning algorithms, can leverage historical data to identify predictive indicators or signals of churn, enabling businesses to intervene proactively and implement targeted retention initiatives.
3-Segmentation and Targeting: Churn analysis often involves segmenting customers based on various attributes, such as demographics, purchase history, usage patterns, or engagement levels. By segmenting customers, businesses can better understand the unique needs and behaviors of different customer groups and tailor retention strategies and marketing efforts accordingly.
4- Identifying At-Risk Customers: Churn data analysis helps businesses identify customers who are most likely to churn based on predefined criteria or predictive models. By identifying at-risk customers early on, businesses can implement targeted interventions, such as personalized offers, proactive outreach, or enhanced customer support, to prevent churn and retain valuable customers.
5- Optimizing Retention Strategies: Churn analysis provides valuable insights into the effectiveness of existing retention strategies and initiatives. By analyzing the impact of different interventions on customer churn rates, businesses can identify which retention tactics are most effective and allocate resources more efficiently to maximize retention efforts.
6-Improving Product and Service Offerings: Churn data analysis can uncover valuable feedback and insights from churned customers regarding product or service shortcomings, dissatisfaction, or unmet needs. By analyzing customer feedback and sentiment data, businesses can identify opportunities for product or service improvements to enhance customer satisfaction and loyalty.
7- Measuring and Monitoring Churn Rate: Churn analysis enables businesses to track and monitor churn rates over time to assess the effectiveness of retention efforts and identify any changes or trends in customer behavior. By regularly measuring churn metrics, businesses can evaluate the impact of interventions and adjust strategies as needed to minimize churn and maximize customer lifetime value.

Results

The dashboards reveals that Only 9.6% of the customer was marked with low satisfaction. The total percentage of churn is 26.5% the majority of which came from customers with short term contract.
